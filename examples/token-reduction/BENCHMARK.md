# Token-Reduction Benchmark - Results

> Generated by `bun run examples/token-reduction/benchmark.ts`. Numbers are
> produced from the actual retrieved content over the committed corpus in `raw/`.
> Tokens are estimated as `ceil(chars / 4)`; exact characters are shown so the
> figures are independently verifiable.

## Corpus

- 8 documents, 17,727 characters (~4,432 tokens total)
- Sharing: 2 org-wide, 4 eng-only, 2 hr-only
- Users: `alice` (eng), `bob` (hr) - each sees only their permitted subset
- Retrieval: top **6** snippets per query

## Headline

- **7.4× fewer tokens** than dumping the whole knowledge base into context.
- **4.8× fewer tokens** even versus dumping only each user's *permitted* subset.
- The reduction grows with corpus size: retrieved size stays ~constant while the dump grows with every document added.

## ACL leak check

Same query - *"What are the compensation bands for senior engineers?"* - asked by the permitted user and the
non-permitted user. The restricted **Compensation Bands 2026** document must appear for
one and never for the other:

| Asked by | Group | Sees "Compensation Bands 2026" in results? |
|---|---|:--:|
| `bob` | hr (permitted) | ✅ yes |
| `alice` | eng (not permitted) | ✅ never |

> `alice` still gets 6 snippets from her *own* permitted docs, but the HR
> compensation document is never among them. A naive "dump the knowledge base"
> baseline would have handed it straight to her.

## What this shows

1. **Token reduction** - answering a question needs a handful of snippets, not the
   whole knowledge base. The bigger the KB, the larger the win, because the
   retrieved size stays roughly constant as documents are added.
2. **No cross-user leak** - the restricted document surfaces only for the permitted
   user (see the ACL leak check). The permission gate produces the candidate set;
   it is not a filter applied after retrieval that you could forget.
3. **Ranking quality** depends on the embedder. This run uses the dependency-free
   hashing embedder, so *which* snippets come back is not yet semantically ranked;
   the token-reduction and ACL guarantees are structural and hold regardless, while
   ranking quality improves with real embeddings (see the README "Status → Next").
