# Hiring Process

This document describes how Acme hires. It is for the people team and any employee acting as an interviewer.

## Principles

We hire for trajectory and judgment over credentials. A strong interview process is fast, respectful of the candidate's time, and consistent across candidates so that decisions are comparable. Every interviewer evaluates against a written rubric and submits a recommendation before seeing anyone else's, so the debrief is not anchored by the first loud opinion.

## Stages

The process has four stages. A recruiter screen confirms basics and mutual interest. A hiring-manager conversation goes deep on motivation and past work. A technical or craft stage is a practical exercise that resembles the actual job, never a trivia quiz or a whiteboard puzzle. A final stage covers values alignment and gives the candidate time with potential peers.

## The debrief

After the loop, interviewers submit independent written feedback with a clear hire or no-hire and the evidence behind it. The hiring manager runs a debrief where the decision is made by weighing evidence, not by averaging scores or counting votes. A single well-argued no-hire on a values concern outweighs several lukewarm yeses.

## Offers

Offers are made at the band for the level, with no sandbagging and no inflating to win a bidding war. We explain the band, the equity, and the philosophy so the candidate understands the system they are joining. We move quickly once we decide, because the best candidates have other options and slowness reads as indecision.

## Candidate experience

Every candidate, including those we decline, should leave with a positive impression of Acme. Respond promptly, never ghost, and give honest timelines. Decline with kindness and, where appropriate, specific encouragement. Today's no-hire is tomorrow's customer, referrer, or stronger re-applicant.

## Bias and fairness

Structured rubrics and independent feedback exist to reduce bias, but they are not sufficient on their own. Interviewers complete fairness training before joining a loop. We track funnel metrics by stage to catch where qualified candidates are dropping out and fix the process, not just exhort people to do better.
