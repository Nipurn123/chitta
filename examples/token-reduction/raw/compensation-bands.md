# Compensation Bands 2026

This document describes how Acme sets pay. It is restricted to the people team and managers in a compensation conversation. Treat the specific numbers as confidential.

## Philosophy

We pay at the seventy-fifth percentile of the market for the role and location tier, and we pay the same band for the same level regardless of how hard someone negotiated. Bands are public internally so that pay is a function of level and impact, not of bargaining. We review compensation once a year and on promotion.

## Levels and bands

Engineering levels run from associate through staff and principal. Associate engineers sit in the entry band. Mid-level engineers occupy the core band that most of the team falls into. Senior engineers move into a higher band with a base range of roughly one hundred eighty to two hundred twenty thousand. Staff engineers occupy a band of roughly two hundred thirty to two hundred eighty thousand. Principal is set case by case.

## Equity

Every employee receives equity at hire, vesting over four years with a one-year cliff. We refresh equity annually each January for people who are performing, so that total ownership grows over time rather than decaying as the initial grant vests. Equity bands scale with level on the same percentile philosophy as cash.

## Location

We use a small number of location tiers rather than a continuous geographic adjustment, to keep the system legible. Moving between tiers adjusts the band but never reduces base pay mid-year; changes apply at the next review. Fully remote employees are mapped to the tier of their metro area.

## Promotions and raises

Promotions move you to the new level's band immediately, not at the next cycle. Annual raises reflect both market movement and individual impact. We do not do counteroffers as a retention tactic, because a system where the loudest exit threat wins is the system we are explicitly trying to avoid.

## Transparency

Managers can see the bands for their reports and discuss them openly. If you believe you are mis-leveled or mis-paid relative to the band, that is a conversation to have directly with your manager and the people team, with data.
