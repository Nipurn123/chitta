# Acme Company Handbook

Acme builds privacy-first AI infrastructure for regulated industries. Our mission is to make permission-aware memory a default primitive for every AI application, not an afterthought bolted on at the end.

## Values

We operate on three values: trust, speed, and ownership. Trust means we never ship a feature that can leak a customer's data across a permission boundary - security is the product, not a setting. Speed means we prefer a working, tested slice today over a perfect design next quarter. Ownership means whoever notices a problem owns getting it fixed or finding the person who can.

## How we work

We are remote-first across nine time zones. Most coordination happens asynchronously in written documents like this one. Meetings are for decisions and relationship-building, not status updates; status lives in the tracker. Every project has a single directly responsible individual, and that name is written down before work starts.

## Time off

All employees get unlimited paid time off with a four-week minimum that managers actively encourage people to hit. We also close the company entirely for the last week of December so that nobody comes back to a backlog built by colleagues who kept working. Parental leave is sixteen weeks at full pay for all new parents regardless of role or tenure.

## Equipment and expenses

Everyone gets a laptop of their choosing up to a set budget, refreshed every three years. Home-office setup has a one-time stipend. Reasonable work expenses are reimbursed without pre-approval up to a monthly cap; above that, ask your manager first. Submit receipts in the expense tool within thirty days.

## Communication norms

Default to public channels over direct messages so context is searchable later. Assume positive intent in writing, where tone is easy to misread. If a thread runs longer than ten messages, switch to a short call and post the summary back. Document decisions where the affected people will find them, which usually means a doc, not a chat scrollback.
